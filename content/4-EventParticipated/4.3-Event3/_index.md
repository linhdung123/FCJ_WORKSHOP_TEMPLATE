# Event Report: "FCAJ Community Day"

### Purpose of the Event
- Sharing real-world experiences and the mindset required for students pursuing careers in Data Analytics.
- Understanding the culture of multinational corporations (MNCs) and global working standards.
- Learning the true role of DevOps and an effective learning roadmap.
- Presenting a scalable URL Shortening Service built on AWS.

- **Dinh Trung Kien** - Lead Developer at a Startup
- **Nguyen Minh Tho** - Student
- **Hieu Nghi** - AI Engineer, AWS Community Builder, AWS Student Builder Group Leader
- **Dat Pham** - Data Analytics Engineer

### Key Highlights

### Real-World Responsibilities of a Data Analytics Engineer
- The responsibilities of a Data Analytics Engineer vary across industries. The speaker shared experiences ranging from developing operational dashboards at Kamereo to working on IoT projects and optimizing manufacturing costs at Colgate-Palmolive.

### Essential Skills for an Analytics Engineer
- Critical thinking.
- Communication skills.
- Problem-solving ability.
- Most importantly, the ability to **"Tell Stories with Data"**, transforming raw numbers into meaningful business insights and actions.

### Five-Stage Growth Model
- A recommended career development roadmap consisting of five stages:
  - Follower
  - Learner
  - Problem Solver
  - System Thinker
  - Super Star

  This model emphasizes continuous personal and professional growth.

### MNC Corporate Culture
- **Standard Recruitment Process:** ATS Screening → Technical Assessment → Technical Interview (STAR Method) → Cultural Fit Interview.
- **No-Blame Post-Mortem:** When system failures occur, organizations focus on identifying the root cause and improving the system instead of blaming individuals.
- **Wakon Yosai Philosophy:** Preserve cultural identity while mastering advanced technologies to compete globally.

### Effective Learning Roadmap for DevOps
- **The True Nature of DevOps:** DevOps is not only about writing CI/CD pipelines or managing Docker and Kubernetes. It is about ensuring reliable system operations and solving real-world operational challenges.
- **Learning Priorities:** Build a strong foundation in Linux, Networking, Python or Golang, and Git before learning specific DevOps tools.
- **DevOps Mindset:** Stay curious, think in terms of systems rather than isolated tasks, automate repetitive work, and leverage AI to enhance productivity instead of replacing critical thinking.
- **Practical Lesson:** Understand *why* before learning *how*, and recognize that communication is an essential part of the job.

### Career Development and Community Support
- **Career Vision:** Landing a job is only the beginning. Career paths such as Solutions Architect, DevOps Engineer, and Software Engineer offer opportunities to build long-term professional growth.
- **Community Support:** Students were encouraged to join programs such as the AWS Student Builder Group (formerly AWS Cloud Clubs) and AWS Community Builder to expand their professional network and connect with the AWS ecosystem.

### URL Shortening Service Demonstration
- **Product Demo:** A scalable URL Shortening Service built on AWS was presented to demonstrate how cloud-native architectures can support high availability and scalability.

### What I Learned
- Job responsibilities vary depending on the industry and business needs, requiring engineers to design systems or analytical reports that optimize business operations and costs.
- Instead of focusing solely on adding more features, it is more important to continuously improve both technical and soft skills by progressing through the five stages of professional growth.
- In DevOps, tools constantly evolve, but fundamental knowledge of Linux, Networking, and Programming remains essential and should always be prioritized.
- DevOps is not simply about completing isolated tasks or building CI/CD pipelines; it is about adopting a system-oriented mindset to ensure reliable operations while automating repetitive work.
- **Be a Good Person, Build a Professional Career, and Contribute to Society:** This philosophy emphasizes understanding one's responsibilities and creating value for both the workplace and the community.

#### Lessons Learned
- AI should be viewed as a tool to enhance human capabilities rather than replace human thinking.
- Actively participating in communities such as the AWS Student Builder Group and AWS Community Builder can significantly expand professional networks and create global career opportunities.
